Wess

Chess, reimagined.

What is Wess?

Wess is a free, open-source chess platform with a twist: you don't just play chess — you build your army first. Before each game, both players draft their starting pieces from a roster of 12 unique piece types within a 42-point budget. The classic army costs 39 points, leaving room to swap in fairy pieces for a strategic edge.

The Pieces

Pawn 1pt
The classic foot soldier. Moves forward, captures diagonally.
Sentinel 2pt
Moves one square orthogonally, captures one square diagonally. A cheap defensive utility.
Knight 3pt
The L-shaped leaper. Jumps over pieces.
Bishop 3pt
Slides diagonally any distance.
Warden 3pt
Leaps exactly 2 squares in any direction, jumping over pieces.
Lancer 4pt
Slides up to 3 squares forward or forward-diagonal. Retreats 1 square.
Rook 5pt
Slides orthogonally any distance. Participates in castling.
Vanguard 6pt
Slides diagonally like a bishop, plus moves (not captures) up to 2 squares orthogonally.
Archbishop 7pt
Combines bishop and knight movement.
Chancellor 8pt
Combines rook and knight movement.
Queen 9pt
Slides in all directions. The most powerful standard piece.
King free
Moves one square in any direction. Mandatory — protect it at all costs.

How It Works

  1. Create or join a game — choose your time control and draft timer.
  2. Draft your army — place pieces on your first 3 rows within the 42-point budget. At least 4 pawns and exactly 1 king required.
  3. Play — both armies are revealed simultaneously. First moves are free (15-second grace period), then clocks start.

Technical Details

Wess is built with TypeScript and Vite with zero runtime dependencies. The chess engine, move generation, and all UI rendering are custom-built from scratch. Multiplayer uses Server-Sent Events over a vanilla Node.js server.

The piece system is fully extensible — adding a new piece requires only a movement definition and an SVG. The engine handles FEN, SAN notation, check/checkmate detection, and all special rules automatically.
